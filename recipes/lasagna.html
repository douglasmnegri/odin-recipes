Lasagna & Lasagna: A recipe for a... Lasagna?

This is a classic homemade recipe for a very special Lasagna. There's hardly a more comforting dish than a hot, freshly baked LASAGNA!

This recipe is composed of beef ragu, bechamel sauce, extraordinary fresh pasta and mozzarela cheese.

Step by Step - Ragu

  1. Heat half the oil in the pressure cooker over medium-high heat. Add 2-3 beef pieces. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until browned.

    Transfer to a large plate.Repeat, in 3-4 batches, with remaining beef.


  2. Heat the remaining oil in the pressure cooker over medium heat. Add celery, carrot, onion and garlic.

    Cook, stirring often, for 3-4 minutes or until soft.


  3. Add the beef, passata and wine, and stir to combine. Dissolve the stock cube in the water.

    Add the stock, bay leaves, oregano and sugar. Season with salt and pepper. Stir to combine.


  4. Seal the pressure cooker. Heat over high heat until steam escapes at a constant rate and there is a regular hissing sound.

    Reduce heat to low. Cook for 40 minutes. Release steam following manufacturer's instructions.


  5. Use tongs to remove beef from the pressure cooker. Shred beef. Return beef to pressure cooker. Stir to combine.

    Add pasta and stir to combine. Divide among serving bowls. Top with parmesan to serve.


Step by Step - Bechamel Sauce

  1. Measure 45 ml (3 tablespoons) of butter and 45 ml (3 tablespoons) of flour per 500 ml (2 cups) of milk.

  2. Melt the butter over medium heat in a saucepan, add 1/2 chopped onion and stir until tender and almost translucent (looks like you can see through them!).

  3. Let the butter and flour mixture cook for 1 minute over medium heat, whisking constantly. That way, your bechamel wonâ€™t taste like flour.

    This step is called making a roux. The roux is used to thicken your milk and give you a nice creamy sauce.


  4. Add the milk and a pinch of nutmeg, stiring constantly with a whisk.

    Bring to a boil (over medium-high heat). If you stop whisking, your sauce will be lumpy.


  5. When the consistency of your sauce becomes thick and creamy (you feel your arm getting tired of stirring!),

    remove from the heat and season with salt and pepper. Taste before seasoning.
